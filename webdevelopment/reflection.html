Personal Reflection

I am a passionate performer.

Throughout my experiences as a piano major in university, I have grown to love communities that appreciate classical music. I found more purpose in spreading the power of music to transcend language, connect people, and inspire others. Listening to any piece is a better experience when shared with an audience. I wish to keep the masterpieces of classical piano alive, even in our quickly evolving society today.

I am an ardent collaborator.

In a similar manner, collaborating with other musicians is a unique and special experience. I have been able to explore this from my experience at Bowdoin Music Festival and with my piano trio. Playing with other instruments opens so many opportunities for me as a pianist to share hidden gems in the repertoire.

I am a growing educator.

I started teaching during my junior year at university, and I love teaching students and guiding them through their own musical journey. My teaching philosophy is to emphasize a positive relationship between you and music. As a performer, it can be easy to fall into frustration and anger during practice. However, this can become incredibly unhealthy and self-harming—instead, I want to inspire through my teaching.

I am always learning.

Even in high school, I always loved expanding my horizons in any subject. I took many courses in preparation for a career in the STEM field, specifically chemistry. In fact, I started university at Mizzou with a double major in chemistry and music. I started undergraduate research, but quickly realized that I felt devoid of life and purpose in the lab. My transition to music gave me more purpose, but I have kept my curiosity and desire for knowledge.
